          <h1 id="Research-Interests">
            <a href="#Research-Interests" class="headerlink" title="Research Interests"></a>
            Research Interests
          </h1>
          <p>
            My primary interests lie broadly in <strong>LLM Reasoning and Synthetic Dataset</strong>,
            <strong>Trustworthy AI</strong>, and <strong>Distributed Algorithms</strong>.
          </p>

          <p>My current research directions include:</p>

          <!-- (1) Reasoning and Synthetic Dataset -->
          <p><strong>(1) Reasoning and Synthetic Dataset</strong></p>
          <ul>
            <li>
              <strong>Small Model Learnability Gap</strong>: Small models do not consistently benefit from long CoT or distillation from large teachers. Instead, they perform better on shorter, simpler reasoning chains that better align with their intrinsic learning capacity. We propose <em>Mix Distillation</em>, a simple yet effective strategy that combines both long and short CoT examples or reasoning from both large and small teachers.
            </li>
            <li>
              <strong><a href="https://huggingface.co/collections/Magpie-Align/magpielm-66e2221f31fa3bf05b10786a">Magpie</a></strong>: We maintained the official Magpie Hugging Face repository and released open-sourced synthetic data generated from open-source LLMs. Our aligned MagpieLM models are still <strong>SOTA</strong> small language models for chat.
            </li>
            <li>
              <strong>SafeChain</strong>: Investigated how long CoT impacts safety and revealed that long CoT does not necessarily enhance safety. We introduced SafeChain, a dataset designed to improve safety alignment while preserving reasoning capabilities.
            </li>
          </ul>

          <!-- (2) Trustworthy AI -->
          <p><strong>(2) Trustworthy AI</strong></p>
          <ul>
            <li>
              <strong>CLEANGEN</strong>: A novel decoding algorithm that defends against various generative backdoor attacks, including advertisement injection, code injection, and malicious content generation. We proposed theoretical proofs to optimize the decoding overhead and efficiency.
            </li>
            <li>
              <strong>Spectral Aggregation</strong>: A novel defense mechanism to aggregate the spectral features of multilayers to detect poisoned samples, restoring the effectiveness of the latent separability assumption in adaptive backdoor attacks.
            </li>
          </ul>

          <!-- (3) Distributed Algorithms -->
          <p><strong>(3) Distributed Algorithms</strong></p>
          <ul>
            <li>
              <strong>Distributed Consensus</strong>: Work includes Voting Validity, Wireless Distributed Consensus, Resource Allocation, and Distributed Consensus Network.
            </li>
          </ul>
