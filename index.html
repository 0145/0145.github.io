Research Interests

sdfbjidi My primary interests lie broadly in LLM reasoning, synthetic dataset and Trustworthy AI. My current research directions include: (1) Reasoning and Synthetic Dataset: Small Model Learnability Gap: small models do not consistently benefit from long CoT or distillation from large teachers. Instead they perform better on shorter, simpler reasoning chains that better align with their intrinsic learning capacity. We propose Mix Distillation, a simple yet effective strategy that by combining long and short CoT examples or reasoning from both large and small teachers. Large Model Paradox: larger models do not necessarily generate better synthetic datasets. We find that the quality of synthetic datasets is closely related to the response generator. MagpieLM: We maintained the official Magpie Hugging Face repository and released open-sourced synthetic data generated from open-source LMs. Our aligned MagpieLM models are still SOTA small language models for chat. SafeChain: Investigated how long CoT impacts safety and revealed that long CoT does not necessarily enhance safety. We introduced SafeChain, a dataset designed to improve the safety alignment while preserving reasoning capabilities. (2) Trustworthy AI: CLEANGEN: a novel decoding algorithm that defenses against various generative backdoor attacks, including advertisement injection, code injection, and malicious content generation. We proposed theoretical proofs to optimize the decoding overhead and efficiency. Spectral Aggregation: a novel defense mechanism to aggregate the spectral features of multilayers to detect poisoned samples, restoring the effectiveness of latent separability assumption in adaptive backdoor attacks. (3)Distributed Algorithms Distributed Consensus. Work includes Voting Validity, Wireless Distributed Consensus, and Distributed Consensus Network.
