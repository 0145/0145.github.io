About Me

I am a second-year PhD student at Network Security Lab at University of Washington, advised by Prof. Radha Poovendran. I also work closely with Dr. Yue Xiang from CMU LTI and Zhangchen Xu from UW. Prior to UW, I obtained my Bachelor degree in Communication Engineering at University of Glasgow (UofG) and University of Electronic Science and Technology of China (UESTC). During my undergrad, I was advised by Prof. Lei Zhang from the University of Glasgow. My CV (updated 12.25, 2022) shows more details.

Research Interests

It is worth noting that there are three different consensus theories known in control, distributed computer systems, and human social psychology. It seems that consensus shows its significance in the philosophy of distributed scenarios. I summarize that they emphasize group intelligence to be dynamic, reliable, and cognitive, respectively. My previous work aimed to explore consensus of computers in distributed intelligent systems for reliability. For my future research, exploring the fundamental philosophy behind three consensus theories, and finding new application scenarios or theory foundations of consensus is an intriguing topic.
